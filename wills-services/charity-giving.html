How Do You Leave Money to a Charity in Your Will?

Choose the Right Charity

Start by asking yourself what charity you’d like to contribute to. This will help prevent confusion when your will is carried out.

Write a Clear Instruction

Clearly write a clause in your will detailing the money or share of the estate you would like to leave in charity’s favour. You can also specify what the money should be used for, if you have any particular plans.

Validate and Inform

Your will is not valid unless signed, dated and witnessed. It’s wise to have at least some close family members aware that you have named the charity and followed through, so there is no hitch executing your wishes.

What do You Need to Know Before You Leave Money to a Charity?

Deciding to leave behind a bequest for a charity is a generous move, but with a few practical precautions, you can help fend off any issues and ensure that your desire is honored. Properly planned, your gift will be clear and impactful:

Is the Charity Properly Identified?

In your will, make sure to include with the charity’s legal name, registration number and address. Small errors can cause confusion or delay to your heirs after your estate is divided.

Did You Place a Designation on the Money?

If you want your gift to benefit a particular project or cause, say so. If not, the charity will direct your donation to where it is most needed.

Will Your Family Know How You Want to Die?

You’re not legally required to tell your relatives, but it’s a good idea. Honest discussions can help ward off confrontations and are an essential element of ensuring that your charitable gift is respected without dispute.
